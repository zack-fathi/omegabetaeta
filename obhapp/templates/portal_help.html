Help & Guide

Logging In

  1. Click Portal in the navigation bar or go directly to /portal/.
  2. Enter your username (first and last name, lowercase, no spaces — e.g. jawadalsahlani) and password.
  3. Your default password is a randomly generated 8-digit number sent to your uniqname@umich.edu email. You will be required to change it on first login.

Portal Home

After logging in you'll see:

  • A welcome message with your name.
  • The Fraternity Calendar with upcoming events, meetings, and socials.

Use the ☰ menu button (top left) to open the sidebar. Your profile badge (top right) links to your account settings.

Sidebar Navigation

Changing Your Password

  1. On your first login with a default password, you will be automatically redirected to change it.
  2. You can also change your password at any time from your Account page.
  3. Scroll down to Change Password.
  4. Enter your current password, then your new password twice.
  5. Click Change Password.
Password Management (Level 1 only): A banner on the Directory page shows how many brothers still need credentials. Click View List to see the status, or click Send All Default Passwords to send credentials to everyone who hasn't received them. You can also manage passwords from a brother's detail page.

Editing Your Profile

  1. Go to your Account page (click your name in the top right).
  2. You can update:
  • Username — your login name
  • Full Name — displayed across the site
  • Profile Picture — click your current profile picture at the top to upload a new one. Hover over it to see a clear preview.
  • Major — your field of study
  • Job — your current job or internship
  • Description — a short bio shown on your profile page
  • Grad Time — your expected graduation month/year
  • Activity Status — choose Active or Inactive from the dropdown
Managing Your Contacts

Below the profile fields is a Contacts section where you can manage your contact info.

  • Add Contact — click to add a new contact entry. Choose a type (Email, Phone, Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.) and enter the value.
  • Primary () — mark one contact as primary. This is the contact shown on your directory card.
  • Public vs Private ( / ) — public contacts appear on the public Brothers page. Private contacts are portal-only.
  • Delete — click the trash icon to remove a contact.

Directory

Browse all brothers organized by line. Each card shows profile picture, name, lion name, and primary contact info. Active/inactive status is shown with a green/red dot.

  • Search — type in the search bar to filter by name, lion name, uniqname, contacts, line number, or member ID
  • Filter by Line — use the line dropdown to show only members from a specific line
  • Filter by Status — use the status dropdown to show only active or inactive members
  • View Details — click any member card to see their full profile with contacts, bio, and all info

Board members (Level 1–4) can also:

  • Edit Member — click the Edit button on a member's detail page to change any field. All edits are logged.
  • Delete Member (Level 1 only) — permanently remove a member from the Danger Zone section at the bottom of the edit view. This action is logged and cannot be undone.

Recruits

All brothers can view the list of people who applied through the public Apply page.

Board members with Level 1–3 (Executive, Vice President, or Director of Recruitment) can also:

  • Set Line Number and Lion Name — fill these in before accepting a recruit
  • Accept / Unaccept — toggle a recruit's acceptance status (both fields must be filled first)
  • Delete — soft-deletes a recruit (moves them to the Deleted section). This action is logged.
  • Deleted Recruits section — expand the section at the bottom to see all previously deleted recruits
  • Restore — bring a deleted recruit back to the active list. This action is also logged.
  • Move Accepted Recruits to Brothers — creates accounts for all accepted recruits with a default password. Every recruit must be either accepted or deleted first — the button will not proceed if any recruits are still pending.

Re-applicants: If someone who was previously deleted applies again with the same uniqname, they will automatically reappear as a new pending recruit.

Board

Shows all current officer roles, their permission levels, and who holds them. All brothers can view this page to see the board structure and permission levels.

Permission Levels

Every board role has a permission level (1–4) that determines what the role holder can do. Lower numbers mean more access. Members with no board role can view all pages but cannot edit anything.

1 Executive Admin, President
  • Full access to all portal features
  • Assign and remove board roles and holders
  • Add and remove custom roles
  • Change permission levels (except Admin/President)
  • Manage site administrators
  • Manage gallery (upload, delete, reorder, carousel)
  • Manage recruits (accept, delete, move to brothers)
  • Edit and delete members in the directory
  • Manage lion names (add, edit, delete)
  • Send and reset passwords
2 Vice President Internal VP, External VP
  • Everything except managing board roles, permission levels, and administrators
  • Manage gallery (upload, delete, reorder, carousel)
  • Manage recruits (accept, delete, move to brothers)

  • Manage lion names (add, edit, delete)
3 Director Director of Recruitment
  • Everything except board roles, administrators, and lion names
  • Manage gallery (upload, delete, reorder, carousel)
  • Manage recruits (accept, delete, move to brothers)
  • Edit member profiles in the directory
4 Board Member All other Director positions (Finance, Internal, PR, Education, Philanthropy, External), Administrator
  • Manage gallery (upload, delete, reorder, carousel)
  • Edit member profiles in the directory
— Member (No Role) All brothers without a board role
  • View all pages (directory, board, gallery, lion names, recruits, messages, log)
  • Edit own profile and change own password

Assigning Board Roles

Level 1 (Executive) only:

  1. Each role is shown as a card with the current holder, permission level badge, and a dropdown to assign a new holder.
  2. Use the dropdown to select an active brother, or choose Remove current holder to clear the position.
  3. Click Save Board Roles to apply all holder changes at once.
Managing Permission Levels

Level 1 (Executive) only:

  • Each role card shows a Level dropdown to change its permission level (1–4).
  • Admin and President are locked at Level 1 and cannot be changed.
  • Changes take effect immediately — no save button needed for permission levels.
Adding & Removing Roles

Level 1 (Executive) only:

  • Add a role — use the "Add New Role" form below the board grid. Choose a name and permission level.
  • Remove a role — click the icon on any role card. Admin and President roles cannot be removed.
Managing Administrators

Level 1 (Executive) only:

  • Add an admin — select any brother from the dropdown and click Add Admin.
  • Remove an admin — click the button on an admin's card. You cannot remove the last admin.
Admin and President both have Level 1 with the same permissions. Admin is a permanent role that is always Level 1.

Lion Names

Shows all lion names, their meanings, and the brothers who hold each name. All brothers can view this page.

Editing meanings (Level 1–2):

  1. Click the icon on a lion name card.
  2. Enter or update the meaning and click Save.

Adding a new lion name (Level 1–2):

  1. Enter the name and optional meaning in the form at the top.
  2. Click Add. The new name will be available for recruits and brothers.

Gallery

All brothers can view the gallery. Board members (Level 1–4) can manage the public photo gallery — upload, edit, reorder, and delete photos.

Uploading photos:

  1. Drag and drop images onto the upload zone, or click it to browse.
  2. Add a description for each photo in the upload queue.
  3. Click Upload All to upload every photo at once.

Managing photos:

  • Reorder () — drag photos by the grip handle to change their display order on the public gallery.
  • Edit description — click the description field on any photo to edit it inline. Changes save when you click away or press Enter.
  • Delete () — permanently removes a photo from the system. This cannot be undone.

Carousel star () — toggle whether a photo appears in the homepage carousel.

Focus control () — adjusts where the carousel crops the photo vertically.

Visibility toggle () — show or hide a photo from the public gallery. Hidden photos can still appear in the carousel.

Log

Shows a history of all actions taken in the portal — profile edits, recruit changes, role assignments, etc. This is view-only and restricted to Level 1–2 (Executive and Vice President).

Messages

All brothers can view messages submitted through the public Contact page — including the sender's name, email, subject, and message.

  • Reply — click the Reply button on any message to send a response. The reply is emailed to the sender and saved in the portal.
  • Multiple replies — you can reply to the same message multiple times. All replies are shown below the original message.

Quick Tips

  • Forgot your password? Use the Reset Password form on the login page. Enter your username and a new temporary password will be emailed to your uniqname@umich.edu address.
  • Profile picture not showing? Make sure you uploaded an image file (PNG, JPG) on the Account page.
  • Can't edit Gallery or change Recruits/Board? These features are restricted to specific permission levels. Check the Board section for details.
  • Login link in email not working on phone? If the "Sign In to Portal" button doesn't work, scroll down in the email — there's a plain text link you can copy and paste into your browser.
  • Files link opens an external Google Drive — bookmark it for quick access.
  • Calendar on phone — the calendar automatically switches to list view on small screens for easier reading.

Calendar Setup Maintainers

The site uses two Google Calendars — one for public events and one for internal events. Both feed into the portal calendar; only the public one appears on the front-facing website. The calendars do not need to be public — access is handled through a service account.

Public Calendar Shown on the public Calendar page and Portal. Use for rush events, socials, community events — anything the public should see.
Portal Calendar Shown in the Portal only. Use for board meetings, chapter meetings, internal deadlines.

Quick links to manage calendars in Google Calendar:

  • Open Public Calendar in Google Calendar
  • Open Portal Calendar in Google Calendar

Adding events: Just add events to the appropriate Google Calendar — they appear on the site automatically. No code changes needed.

Service Account Setup

The site uses a Google Cloud service account to read calendar events server-side. This means the calendars stay private — only the service account has access. Here's how to set it up:

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console .
  2. Create a new project (or select an existing one).
  3. Go to APIs & Services → Library, search for Google Calendar API, and click Enable.
  4. Go to APIs & Services → Credentials.
  5. Click Create Credentials → Service Account.
  6. Give it a name (e.g. obh-calendar) and click Done.
  7. Click on the service account you just created, go to the Keys tab.
  8. Click Add Key → Create new key → JSON. A .json file will download.
  9. Place the JSON file on the server (e.g. /path/to/service-account.json).
  10. Set the path in the .env file: SERVICE_ACCOUNT_FILE=/path/to/service-account.json
  11. Restart the server.
Sharing Calendars with the Service Account

After creating the service account, you need to share each Google Calendar with it. The service account has an email address that looks like name@project-id.iam.gserviceaccount.com — you can find it on the service account's details page in Google Cloud Console.

  1. Open Google Calendar in the account that owns the calendars.
  2. Click the ⋮ (three dots) next to a calendar name → Settings and sharing.
  3. Under Share with specific people or groups, click Add people and groups.
  4. Paste the service account email and set permission to "See all event details".
  5. Repeat for both the Public and Portal calendars.
The calendars remain private. Only the service account can read them — no one else can see them unless you explicitly share.
Changing Calendars

To switch to different Google Calendars, update the IDs in the .env file:

  • PUBLIC_CALENDAR_ID — the Calendar ID for public events
  • PORTAL_CALENDAR_ID — the Calendar ID for internal/board events

You can find a calendar's ID in Google Calendar under Settings → Integrate calendar → Calendar ID. Remember to share the new calendar with the service account email. Then restart the server.
