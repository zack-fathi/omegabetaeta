Help & Guide

Logging In

  1. Click Portal in the navigation bar or go directly to /portal/.
  2. Enter your username (first and last name, lowercase, no spaces — e.g. jawadalsahlani) and password.
  3. Your default password is a randomly generated 8-digit number sent to your uniqname@umich.edu email. You will be required to change it on first login.

Portal Home

After logging in you'll see:

  • A welcome message with your name.
  • The Fraternity Calendar with upcoming events, meetings, and socials.

Use the ☰ menu button (top left) to open the sidebar. Your profile badge (top right) links to your account settings.

Changing Your Password

  1. On your first login with a default password, you will be automatically redirected to change it.
  2. You can also change your password at any time from your Account page.
  3. Scroll down to Change Password.
  4. Enter your current password, then your new password twice.
  5. Click Change Password.
Password Management (Admin/President only): A banner on the Directory page shows how many brothers haven't been sent credentials or haven't changed their password yet. Click View List to see a detailed table with each brother's email-sent and password-changed status, or click Send All Default Passwords to send credentials to everyone who hasn't received them. You can also manage individual passwords from a brother's detail page.

Editing Your Profile

  1. Go to your Account page (click your name in the top right).
  2. You can update:
  • Username — your login name
  • Full Name — displayed across the site
  • Profile Picture — upload a photo of yourself
  • Major — your field of study
  • Job — your current job or internship
  • Description — a short bio shown on your profile page
  • Contacts — how people can reach you (e.g. Instagram, phone, email)
  • Grad Time — your expected graduation month/year
  • Active — whether you're currently active

Click Update Profile to save. Your photo and name appear on the public Brothers page and in the portal Directory.

Directory

Browse all brothers organized by line. Each card shows profile picture, name, lion name, line number, ID, uniqname, and active/inactive status (green/red dot).

  • Search — type in the search bar to filter by name, lion name, uniqname, email, line number, or member ID
  • Filter by Line — use the line dropdown to show only members from a specific line
  • Filter by Status — use the status dropdown to show only active or inactive members
  • View Details — click any member card to see their full profile with all info

Board members and admins can also:

  • Edit Member — click the Edit button on a member's detail page to change any field (username, name, uniqname, major, job, line info, profile picture, active status, etc.). All edits are logged.
  • Delete Member (Admin only) — permanently remove a member from the Danger Zone section at the bottom of the edit view. This action is logged and cannot be undone.

Recruits

All brothers can view the list of people who applied through the public Apply page.

If you have the right role (Admin, President, or Director of Recruitment), you can also:

  • Set Line Number and Lion Name — fill these in before accepting a recruit
  • Accept / Unaccept — toggle a recruit's acceptance status (both fields must be filled first)
  • Delete — soft-deletes a recruit (moves them to the Deleted section). This action is logged.
  • Deleted Recruits section — expand the section at the bottom to see all previously deleted recruits
  • Restore — bring a deleted recruit back to the active list. This action is also logged.
  • Move Accepted Recruits to Brothers — creates accounts for all accepted recruits with a default password. Every recruit must be either accepted or deleted first — the button will not proceed if any recruits are still pending.

Re-applicants: If someone who was previously deleted applies again with the same uniqname, they will automatically reappear as a new pending recruit.

Board

Shows all current officer roles and who holds them, along with site administrators.

Assigning board roles (President or Admin only):

  1. Each role is shown as a card with the current holder's name and photo.
  2. Use the dropdown on any role to select a new active brother, or choose Remove current holder to clear the position.
  3. Click Save Board Roles to apply all changes at once.

Managing administrators (Admin only):

  • Add an admin — select any brother from the dropdown and click Add Admin.
  • Remove an admin — click the button on an admin's card. You cannot remove the last admin.
Only active brothers appear in board role dropdowns. Admins can be any brother (including inactive/alumni) since they maintain the site.

Lion Names

Shows all lion names, their meanings, and the brothers who hold each name.

Editing meanings (President or Admin only):

  1. Click the icon on a lion name card.
  2. Enter or update the meaning and click Save.

Adding a new lion name (President or Admin only):

  1. Enter the name and optional meaning in the form at the top.
  2. Click Add. The new name will be available for recruits and brothers.

Gallery Management Board Only

Go to Gallery in the sidebar to manage the public photo gallery.

Uploading photos:

  1. Drag and drop images onto the upload zone, or click it to browse.
  2. Add a description for each photo in the upload queue.
  3. Click Upload All to upload every photo at once.

Managing photos:

  • Reorder — drag photos by the grip handle () to change their display order on the public gallery.
  • Edit description — click the description text on any photo to edit it inline. Changes save automatically when you click away or press Enter.
  • Delete — click the trash icon () to permanently remove a photo.
All changes take effect immediately on the public Gallery page.

Log

Shows a history of all actions taken in the portal — profile edits, recruit changes, role assignments, etc. This is view-only.

Messages

All brothers can view messages submitted through the public Contact page — including the sender's name, email, subject, and message.

  • Reply — click the Reply button on any message to open the reply form. Type your response and click Send Reply. The reply is emailed to the sender (with the original message quoted for context) and added to the conversation thread.
  • Multiple replies — you can reply to the same message multiple times. All replies are shown in a thread below the original message, in chronological order.
  • No page reload — after sending a reply, a green confirmation appears and the reply is added to the thread instantly without refreshing the page.
  • Email threading — replies are sent from the organization email, so when the sender replies back it goes directly to the inbox. The original message is included in the email so both sides can follow the conversation.

Quick Tips

  • Forgot your password? Ask a brother with Admin access to reset it in the database.
  • Profile picture not showing? Make sure you uploaded an image file (PNG, JPG) on the Account page.
  • Can't edit Gallery or change Recruits/Board? These features are restricted to specific board roles.

Calendar Setup Maintainers

The site uses two Google Calendars — one for public events and one for internal events. Both feed into the portal calendar; only the public one appears on the front-facing website. The calendars do not need to be public — access is handled through a service account.

CalendarShown OnUse For
Public Calendar Public Calendar page and Portal Rush events, socials, community events — anything the public should see
Portal Calendar Portal only Board meetings, chapter meetings, internal deadlines

Quick links to manage calendars in Google Calendar:

  • Open Public Calendar in Google Calendar
  • Open Portal Calendar in Google Calendar

Adding events: Just add events to the appropriate Google Calendar — they appear on the site automatically. No code changes needed.

Service Account Setup

The site uses a Google Cloud service account to read calendar events server-side. This means the calendars stay private — only the service account has access. Here's how to set it up:

  1. Go to the Google Cloud Console .
  2. Create a new project (or select an existing one).
  3. Go to APIs & Services → Library, search for Google Calendar API, and click Enable.
  4. Go to APIs & Services → Credentials.
  5. Click Create Credentials → Service Account.
  6. Give it a name (e.g. obh-calendar) and click Done.
  7. Click on the service account you just created, go to the Keys tab.
  8. Click Add Key → Create new key → JSON. A .json file will download.
  9. Place the JSON file on the server (e.g. /path/to/service-account.json).
  10. Set the path in the .env file: SERVICE_ACCOUNT_FILE=/path/to/service-account.json
  11. Restart the server.
Sharing Calendars with the Service Account

After creating the service account, you need to share each Google Calendar with it. The service account has an email address that looks like name@project-id.iam.gserviceaccount.com — you can find it on the service account's details page in Google Cloud Console.

  1. Open Google Calendar in the account that owns the calendars.
  2. Click the ⋮ (three dots) next to a calendar name → Settings and sharing.
  3. Under Share with specific people or groups, click Add people and groups.
  4. Paste the service account email and set permission to "See all event details".
  5. Repeat for both the Public and Portal calendars.
The calendars remain private. Only the service account can read them — no one else can see them unless you explicitly share.
Changing Calendars

To switch to different Google Calendars, update the IDs in the .env file:

  • PUBLIC_CALENDAR_ID — the Calendar ID for public events
  • PORTAL_CALENDAR_ID — the Calendar ID for internal/board events

You can find a calendar's ID in Google Calendar under Settings → Integrate calendar → Calendar ID. Remember to share the new calendar with the service account email. Then restart the server.
